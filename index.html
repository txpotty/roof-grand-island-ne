Is Your Roof Asking for Help? Signs You Need Roofing Services…ASAP!

Sometimes, your roof can’t talk, but it definitely sends signals, many even obvious ones. Being aware of these signs can save you from larger headaches. Identifying roofing problems early can prevent extensive damage and expensive repairs. Here are some common indicators that your roof may need attention:

Call Now: (857) 201-5457

Leaking Roof

The Problem: Water stains on your ceiling or walls, or actively dripping water, are clear signs of a leak. Leaks can compromise the structure of your home, leading to mold growth and rot, which are health hazards and costly to repair.

Our Solution: We’ll identify the source of the leak and provide a durable repair solution, preventing further water damage and ensuring a dry, safe home environment.

Missing or Damaged Shingles

The Problem: Shingles that are cracked, curled, or missing expose your roof to the elements. This can lead to water damage, premature aging of the roof, and decreased energy efficiency.

Our Solution: We replace damaged or missing shingles with high-quality materials, restoring the integrity of your roof and protecting your home from the elements.

Granules in the Gutters

The Problem: Granules are the tiny pebbles that coat asphalt shingles. If you find a significant amount of granules in your gutters, it indicates that your shingles are deteriorating, and their protective layer is wearing away. This shortens the lifespan of your roof.

Our Solution: We’ll assess the condition of your shingles and recommend either a repair or a full roof replacement, depending on the extent of the damage. This will protect your home and extend the life of your roof.

Sagging Roof Deck

The Problem: A sagging roof deck is a serious issue. It indicates that the underlying structures of your roof are weak or damaged, possibly by water or rot. A sagging roof can lead to a collapse, posing a significant safety risk.

Our Solution: We’ll thoroughly inspect the structure of your roof and provide the necessary repairs or replacement to ensure the safety and stability of your home.

Increased Energy Bills

The Problem: If you notice your energy bills are unusually high, it could be a sign that your roof is not properly insulating your home. Damaged or deteriorating roofing can allow heat to escape in the winter and enter in the summer, putting a strain on your HVAC system. A new roof can lead to huge savings!

Our Solution: We’ll evaluate the insulation and ventilation of your roof and provide solutions to improve energy efficiency, such as adding insulation or repairing damaged roofing materials.

Moss or Algae Growth

The Problem: Moss and algae can grow on roofs in damp, shaded areas. They retain moisture, which can damage roofing materials over time, leading to rot and leaks AND it doesn’t look good.

Our Solution: We’ll clean your roof to remove moss and algae and apply a protective treatment to prevent future growth, prolonging the life of your roof.

Age of Your Roof

The Problem: Most roofs have a lifespan of 20-25 years. If your roof is nearing the end of its life expectancy, it’s important to have it inspected to assess its condition. Even if there are no visible signs of damage, the materials may be deteriorating.

Our Solution: We’ll conduct a comprehensive roof inspection to determine its condition and recommend the most appropriate course of action, whether it’s a repair or a full replacement.

Damaged Flashing

The Problem: Flashing is the metal material installed around chimneys, vents, and skylights to prevent water from entering your home. If the flashing is damaged or corroded, it can cause leaks.

Our Solution: We’ll inspect and repair or replace damaged flashing, ensuring a watertight seal around these vulnerable areas.

Animal Infestation

The Problem: Squirrels, birds, and other animals can cause significant damage to your roof by nesting in gaps or chewing on roofing materials. Plus nobody wants that!

Our Solution: We’ll identify and remove any animal infestations and repair the damage they’ve caused, sealing any entry points to prevent future problems.

Rotting Wood

The Problem: Rotting wood can indicate water damage and failing shingles. This is especially an issue around any edging or areas the connect to the walls.

Our Solution: We’ll identify the areas that are rotting, replace the damaged wood, and find a way to patch the current issue. This helps prevent any future moisture entering.

Frequently Asked Roofing Questions

We understand that roofing projects come with many questions. Here are some common concerns we can help you clarify before moving ahead.

Q: How Long Does a New Roof Installation Take?

A: The length of a roof installation project depends on several factors, including the size of your roof, the type of materials being used, and weather conditions. Typically, a standard roof replacement can take anywhere from one to three days. We’ll provide you with a more accurate timeline after assessing your specific needs.

Q: What is the Best Type of Roofing Material for Grand Island’s Climate?

A: Grand Island experiences a range of weather conditions, from hot summers to cold winters, so durable and weather-resistant roofing materials are essential. Asphalt shingles are a popular choice due to their affordability and versatility. Metal roofing is another excellent option, offering superior durability and longevity. Our experts can help you choose the best material based on your budget and preferences.

Q: How Often Should I Have My Roof Inspected?

A: It’s a good idea to have your roof inspected at least once a year, preferably in the spring or fall. Regular inspections can help identify potential problems early on, preventing costly repairs down the road. It’s also important to have your roof inspected after severe weather events, such as storms with high winds or hail.
